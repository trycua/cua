---
title: "Services"
description: "How a process listening on a port inside a sandbox is exposed and reached from outside the sandbox."
---

A **service** is any process listening on a port inside a sandbox — a web app, an API, a database, or a tool the task starts. Because a sandbox is isolated, a service is only reachable once it is explicitly exposed.

## Exposing a service

An image declares the ports a sandbox will listen on, and the sandbox exposes those ports so they can be reached from outside the machine.

## Reaching a service

A tunnel forwards an exposed port to your local machine or another process, so you can connect to a service running inside the sandbox as if it were local.

## Services and isolation

Exposing a service does not break sandbox isolation. Only the ports you expose are reachable, and they are reachable only for as long as the sandbox lives.
