---
title: 'Fleet autoscaling'
description: 'How Fleet balances claim demand, warm sandbox capacity, and the cost of idle replicas.'
---

Fleet autoscaling is the relationship between demand for claims and the compatible sandbox capacity a
pool maintains. `initialPoolSize` establishes starting capacity. With autoscaling enabled,
`autoscaling.minPoolSize` sets the lower capacity floor and `maxPoolSize` sets the upper bound.
These fields govern capacity targets; available capacity remains a status and readiness result.
Outstanding claims consume ready capacity, while an unclaimed ready replica remains available for
the next claimant.

## Demand has a provisioning path

When demand is greater than available warm capacity, a claim may wait for a replica to be created
or returned to readiness. That path can include image retrieval, VM provisioning, guest boot, and
the readiness check defined by the pool. Its duration depends on the image, infrastructure, guest
workload, and current conditions, so Fleet concepts do not promise a fixed claim or boot time.

Readiness matters at the end of that path. A running VM is not necessarily a useful sandbox if the
service a workload needs has not started. The same distinction applies after a reset: a claim can
be bound while the guest is rebooting and its service remains unavailable.

## Warm capacity is an explicit tradeoff

More warm capacity can reduce the work between a claim and a ready sandbox, but leaves more idle
resources allocated. Less warm capacity reduces idle cost but makes bursts more likely to wait for
provisioning and readiness. Scale-down has the same tension: removing idle replicas saves resources
but leaves less immediately available capacity for later demand.

`maxPoolSize` bounds the amount of capacity that autoscaling can add. That bound is not a promise
that all capacity below it is instantly provisioned, and it does not replace readiness. The practical limit is the intersection of configured targets, warm
availability, provisioning progress, and the compatibility requirements of the pending claims.

Autoscaling changes capacity; [claims](/concepts/fleet/claims) still provide the exclusive lease,
and [services](/concepts/fleet/services) still determine when the leased workload is reachable.
